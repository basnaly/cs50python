After reading some articles and travel blogs about accommodation, I realized that the best approach is to book a hotel when:
1. Transportation The hotel offers easy access to the metro or train stations in cities that have them, and to bus stops in cities without metro or train services.
2. Area Select the safest area within the city.
3. Attractions Opt for a hotel located in the city center, close to popular attractions.

There are three main types of public transportation in Kobe: trains, buses, and bullet trains. Lets check them out one by one.
Train Kobe has many kinds of train: subway, JR, Sanyo railway, Hankyu railway, Hanshin railway, and monorail
Subway There are three main types of subway in Kobe; Seishin-Yamate line, Hokushin line, Kaigan line. Seishin-Yamate line connects from Seishin-chuo station to Shinkobe station. Hokushin line connects from Shinkobe station to Tanigami station. Kaigan line connects from Shinnagata station to Sannomiya-Hanadokei-mae station.
JR Kobe has JR Kobe line. JR Kobe line is from Himeji station to Osaka station. JR has three types of trains; Local (stops at every station), Rapid Service (stops at some stations), and Special Rapid Service (stops at Kobe station and Sannomiya.
Sanyo, Hankyu, Hanshin line These trains connect from Himeji station to Osaka-Umeda station. Sanyo railway runs from Himeji station to Shinkaichi station. Hankyu and Hanshin railway runs from Shinkaichistation to Osaka-Umeda station. Kousoku-Kobe station and Kobe-Sannomiya station have both Hankyu and Hanshin line.
Monorail There are two kinds of Monorail in Kobe; Port Liner and Rokko Liner. The former one connects with Sannomiya, Port Island, and Kobe airport. The latter one connects with Sumiyoshi station and Rokko Island. Monorail does not have local or rapid train. Each train stops at every station.
Bus Many kinds of buses are available in Kobe. There are Kobe-city bus and Sanyo bus.

There are a lot of interesting places to visit in Kobe and outside of the city.
Here are some of them:
  1. Ikuta shrine.
  2. Kobe China Town, one of the biggest China towns in Japan.
  3. Kobe Harborland and Meriken Park, see the iconic Kobe Port Tower.
  4. Rokkosan, breathtaking view over Kobe.
  5. Kobe Nunobiki Herb Gardens, seasonal flowers and ropeway ride.
  6. Kobe Kitano Ijinkan, foreign influenced architectures from 19th century.
  7. Nada Sake District, visit some Japanese sake brewery.
and much more...
